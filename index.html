My Story & Impact

Building Finance Systems That Speak

ACA · MSc Finance & Investment (Distinction, Top 5%) · Harvard Business School Excellence Award 2024

Teslim is a Chartered Accountant (ACA) and Financial Data Analyst who believes numbers are not just figures on a page — they are systems of truth. When used well, they expose reality and guide better decisions. When used poorly, they can disguise risk, create illusions of profit, and quietly drain value.

The road to finance wasn't straight. Teslim applied to study Accounting but was instead offered Human Resources. For many, that could have been the end of the story. For him, it was the beginning.

While completing that degree, he refused to be limited by circumstance. He pursued his real passion in parallel — working through AAT, advancing into ACA, and qualifying before graduation. That decision taught two lessons that continue to define his career:

  • Careers aren't handed out neatly — they are built.
  • Life is 10% of what happens and 90% of how we react to it.

These lessons shaped him into more than an accountant — someone who builds finance systems from the ground up: frameworks designed to test assumptions, expose reality, and give leaders the clarity and confidence to act.

What I Do

Over more than a decade, working at the crossroads of finance, accounting, and data analytics — helping organisations move from confusion to clarity:

As an Accountant and Finance Analyst: building management reporting frameworks, implementing internal controls, and stabilising cash flows in businesses that were running blind.

As a Financial Data Analyst: designing SQL ETL pipelines, developing Power BI dashboards, and applying statistical models that turned raw data into foresight and decision-ready insights.

As Treasurer of a UK-based charity: introducing dashboards and controls that achieved 100% audit compliance, strengthening donor and trustee confidence.

Holding an MSc in Finance & Investment Banking (Distinction, Top 5%) from the University of Hertfordshire and recognised with the Harvard Business School Excellence Award (2024). I don't just record numbers. I design systems that make them speak, guide, and shape the future.

Ten Key Transformations

These are not abstract ideas. They are lived experiences — moments where finance stopped being about reports and became the lens through which leaders saw the truth. Each story is a stage of the journey: building systems, restoring confidence, driving strategy, and ensuring governance.

01

🏗️ Building Finance Systems from the Ground Up

The Challenge

When first stepping into a fast-growing trading business, the numbers were telling two very different stories. The profit and loss reports looked promising, yet the bank account told a harsher truth: cash was consistently short. The owner looked down at the figures, then up — clearly frustrated — and asked:

"If we're making a profit, why don't we have any cash?"
What Was Done

A tailored chart of accounts was designed, separating trade versus cash discounts. Bin cards were introduced and IAS 2 applied for correct inventory valuation. A cash book was built with approval processes embedded. Complete management accounts were produced — P&L, balance sheet, and cash flow statement — giving the business its first complete financial picture.

Impact

The analysis revealed that receivables and obsolete inventory were silently draining working capital. Cash flow stabilised and aligned with reported profits. Decisions shifted from guesswork to fact-based strategy. The owner finally had answers — not just numbers.

02

📊 Forecasting That Leaders Could Trust

The Challenge

Forecasting was little more than guesswork. One forecast predicted ₦500M; the actual result came in at ₦420M. The variance was so wide that an executive challenged the entire process:

"If we keep missing this badly, why should we believe any forecast at all?"
What Was Done

SQL ETL pipelines were built for automated data processing, eliminating manual errors at the source. Z-score analysis was applied to remove statistical outliers that were skewing projections. Regression models and seasonality adjustments were introduced, and Power BI dashboards with variance analysis gave leadership real-time visibility into forecast performance versus actuals.

Impact

Year-end revenue exceeded ₦600M against a ₦420M baseline — a transformation that silenced the sceptics. Executives shifted from debating the accuracy of numbers to focusing on strategy. Leadership confidence in forecasting was restored, and the finance function earned its seat at the strategy table.

03

📆 Consistency in Month-End and Board Reporting

The Challenge

Month-end felt like a race against time. Reports arrived late, figures changed after they had already been seen, and credibility in the finance function was eroding. The tension peaked when a board member sighed:

"Why do these reports keep changing after we've already seen them?"
What Was Done

A structured close checklist was built and mapped to IFRS and UK GAAP requirements. Reconciliations were standardised with cut-off rules that eliminated ambiguity over timing. Full ownership was taken of the P&L, balance sheet, and cash flow statements. Board-ready financial packs were designed — clean, consistent, and structured for decision-making rather than just compliance.

Impact

Five consecutive years of on-time, error-free reporting followed. Restatements were eliminated entirely. Board confidence was restored and meetings shifted from debating accuracy to shaping strategy. The finance team moved from firefighting to leadership.

04

🤝 Driving Transparency in the Non-Profit Sector

The Challenge

Serving as Treasurer of a UK-based registered charity, the stakes were higher than in a commercial business — restricted funds must be used exactly as intended, and any deviation can damage donor trust and regulatory standing. A board member posed the question that cut to the heart of it:

"Can we prove, right now, that every restricted fund has been used exactly as intended?"
What Was Done

Restricted and unrestricted funds were separated with full audit trails for every transaction. Board-level dashboards were built to show reserves, programme spending, and compliance at a glance. Financial statements were aligned with UK charity accounting and audit standards. External reviews were overseen and the audit process managed end to end.

Impact

100% audit compliance was achieved — with no qualifications. Donor and trustee confidence strengthened measurably. Real-time visibility of the charity's financial health enabled more confident governance decisions, and the board could answer that critical question with complete assurance.

05

⚡ Power BI That Managers Actually Use

The Challenge

The dashboards already existed — but nobody used them. Refreshes failed without warning. Drill-downs froze mid-click. A manager tried pulling branch performance data during a critical meeting, only for the system to lock up entirely. Frustrated and embarrassed, he said:

"If I can't get the report in time, I may as well stick to Excel."
What Was Done

The entire data model was re-engineered into a clean star schema. A composite model was implemented to balance query speed with large dataset handling. Keys were indexed and data types optimised to eliminate unnecessary memory overhead. Reliable, scheduled automated refresh processes were deployed, replacing the brittle manual approach that had been failing silently.

Impact

Reports now loaded in under 15 seconds. Full adoption was achieved across the organisation — not because it was mandated, but because the tool actually worked. Strategy meetings shifted from presenting static slides to collaborative, real-time analysis. The phrase "I may as well stick to Excel" was never heard again.

06

📉 From Losses to Market Leadership

The Challenge

Month after month, the reports came back in red. The survival strategy was cutting prices — but the irony was cruel. The more the business sold, the more it lost. The COO leaned forward, frustration written on his face:

"If we're selling more, why are we losing more?"
What Was Done

Break-even analysis was applied to every product and branch, and the margin of safety was introduced as a core decision-making tool. Unprofitable products and underperforming branches were identified — the ones draining cash that management had been propping up with hope rather than evidence. Marketing strategy was redesigned in partnership with the commercial team, introducing loyalty schemes and product bundling to improve yield. Branch dashboards were built for weekly break-even monitoring, putting live visibility in the hands of those responsible for performance.

Impact

Profits rose 8% in a single quarter and doubled by the second. Branches once facing closure returned to profitability. The business repositioned from price-war survivor to segment leader — because the decisions that drove the turnaround were grounded in data, not instinct.

07

🗂️ From Spreadsheet Chaos to Trusted Insight

The Challenge

The problem surfaced in a meeting when two managers argued over the same metric. One insisted debtor days were 45. The other said 60. Neither was lying — they had simply cleaned the same raw data in different ways, producing figures that looked official but measured different things. Finally one asked:

"So which of us is correct?"

The real problem was spreadsheet gymnastics: raw data exported from the system, then manually cleaned in isolation by each department with no shared logic and no single version of truth.

What Was Done

An automated ETL pipeline was implemented to replace the manual consolidation process entirely. Accounts, IDs, and business logic were standardised across all departments. Structured SQL tables with reconciliation checks were built to enforce data integrity at the source. The output was integrated with Power BI dashboards refreshed daily — so every department pulled from the same verified dataset, every time.

Impact

Report preparation time fell by 80%. Error rates dropped by 30%. Meetings shifted from debating which version of a number was correct to acting on one shared, trusted dataset. Finance stopped being a bottleneck and became a source of truth — and those two managers never had that argument again.

08

💼 Optimising Payroll and Forecasting Accuracy

The Challenge

Small, persistent errors were eroding trust. Payroll mistakes — minor in isolation — left staff quietly questioning the reliability of the finance function. One team member summed up the mood with a comment that stung precisely because it was fair:

"If we can't even get payroll right, how can we trust the forecasts?"
What Was Done

Validation rules and automated reconciliations were introduced into the payroll process, eliminating the manual touch points where errors crept in. Exception reports were built to flag anomalies before they reached staff — catching problems at source rather than after the fact. The forecasting model was refined using cleaner datasets, and rolling forecasts were implemented to replace the static annual plan with continuously updated projections.

Impact

Payroll errors were reduced by 25%. Forecast accuracy improved by 15%. More importantly, the reputation of the finance function strengthened organisation-wide — because the challenge had been taken seriously, and the numbers that came out of finance became something people could rely on.

09

💰 Transforming Treasury into a Strategic Function

The Challenge

Treasury was treated as reactive back-office administration — payments in, payments out, nothing more. The organisation had no real-time view of its cash position. When the CEO asked a question that should have had an immediate answer, the silence in the room was telling:

"How much cash do we actually have available today?"
What Was Done

A daily cash visibility dashboard was built, giving leadership an accurate real-time picture of available liquidity. A rolling 13-week cash flow forecast was introduced, extending the organisation's planning horizon beyond the immediate. Liquidity management was optimised by matching inflows with outflow timing — reducing unnecessary borrowing. Surplus cash was deployed into safe short-term instruments to generate yield without exposing the organisation to risk.

Impact

Borrowing costs were reduced and cash alignment improved. Treasury moved from a reactive function to a forward-looking strategic capability. Leadership could answer the CEO's question with confidence — and the organisation stopped being surprised by its own financial position.

10

💧 From Liquidity Panic to Confidence

The Challenge

The signs were everywhere. Staff were openly asking whether the organisation was shutting down. Salaries felt insecure. Loan renewals hung by a thread. Unpaid tax liabilities piled higher each month. What had started as a cash management issue had become a trust crisis — and the finance function needed to act decisively or the damage would become irreversible.

What Was Done

A receivables dashboard was built to identify and flag chronic late payers — surfacing the pattern of overdue debt that had been hiding in plain sight. Negotiations with overdue customers were led directly, and early payment incentives were introduced to accelerate collections. Creditor payments were staggered to match inflow timing, preventing cash from leaving before it arrived. A transparent recovery plan was presented to the bank, demonstrating control and providing the assurance needed to secure continued support.

Impact

Collections improved and liquidity stabilised. Loan renewals were secured and tax liabilities were brought under managed repayment. Staff confidence and trust in finance were restored — because when the situation demanded it, the finance function stepped forward as a stabilising force, not a passive observer.
